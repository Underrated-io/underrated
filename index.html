No, Zombies Are Not Your Friends, & Do Not Believe Them Even If They Say They Are

Alexander Boople

It may be surprising to you, but zombies are *not* your friends. They may seem nice and may moan at you from the lips of your loved ones, but they are only trying to lull you into a false sense of security.


They know they look like your long dead Uncle Ernie and that you left your admiration for his pickle jar collection unsaid before his untimely reanimation. But this is how they trick you.

They get you to think that _maybe_ some part of the Aunt Mildred, who baked you cookies every day after school when all the other PhD candidates called your work silly and foolish and told you you‘d never amount to anything more than an untenured adjunct, is still in there and just might start baking again at any moment if you’d just come within arm’s reach.


They want you to feel that maybe, just maybe that girl down the street who beat you up every single day of your adult life will finally realize that you are much cooler than your pocket protector makes you out to be. That she will finally embrace you for the wonderful person that you are and stop telling her friends that you smell like an uncomplimentary mix of eggs and old-person ointment.


Do not believe them!


They lie!!!!!!


They only want to eat your brains and possibly elevate their apocalyptic social standing by bringing more zombies into the horde. You are *not* your misguided wishes for love and affection. You are a person with thoughts and hopes and dreams. Don’t throw them away by trusting some infected guy from your hometown who was the epitome of cool and now says through his moans that he wants to be your friend.


Stay strong, Alexander—I mean, _yooou_. Just because he’s shown up to finally tell you he was wrong for giving you atomic wedgies and telling everyone that you wet your pants in eighth grade and that’s what you’ve longed for every moment since high school ended doesn’t mean that it has happened or that he doesn’t have ulterior motives.


Hey, Brad, what are you doing—


It’s true?


You want to tell everyone you’re my best friend?


Why, yes, I will gladly come within arm’s reach to hear what you are mumbling…
