No, Zombies Are Not Your Friends, Do Not Believe Them Even If They Say They Are

by Dr. Boople

It may be surprising to you, but zombies are not your friends. They may seem nice and may moan at you from the lips of your love ones, but they are only trying to lull you into a false sense of security

They know they look like your long dead Uncle Ernie and that you left your admiration for his pickle jar collection unsaid before his untimely reanimation. But this is how they trick you.

They get you to think that maybe some part of the Aunt Mildred, who baked you cookies every day after school when all the other PhD candidates called your work silly and foolish and told you you'd never amount to anything more than an untenured adjunct, is still in there and just might start baking again at any moment if you'd just come within arms reach.

They want you to feel that maybe, just maybe that girl down the street who beat you up every single day of your adult life will finally realize that you are much cooler that your pocket protector makes you out to be. That she will finally embrace you for the wonderful peson that you are and stop telling her friends that you smell like an uncomplimentary mix of eggs and old-person ointment.

Do not believe them!

They lie!!!!!!

They only want to eat your brains and possibly elevate their apocalyptic social standing by bringing more zombies into the horde. You are not your misguided wishes for love and affection. You are a person with thoughts and hopes and dreams. Don't throw them away by trusting some infected guy from your hometown who was the epitome of coll and now says through his moans that he wants to be your friend.
